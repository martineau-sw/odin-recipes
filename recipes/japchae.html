Japchae

Description

Korean glass noodles burst with flavor and blend beautifully with protein and vegetables.

Ingredients

  • Korean Dang Myun Noodles, 1/2 pound
  • Sesame Oil, 2 1/2 teaspoons
  • Soy Sauce, 2 tablespoons
  • White Sugar, 2 teaspoons
  • Vegetable Oil, 1 tablespoon
  • 2 Carrots, match-stick cut
  • Asparagus, thinly sized, 1/2 pound
  • Onions, thinly sliced, 3/4 cup
  • Garlic, minced, 2 cloves
  • 3 Green Onions, sliced 1 inch pieces
  • Soaked Soft Shiitake Mushrooms, sliced, 1/2 cup
  • Sesame Seeds, 1 tablespoon

Directions

  1. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Add Korean Dang Myun Noodes and, stirring occasionally, cook al dente from 4 to 5 minutes. Drain, then rinse with cold water. Toss noodles with sesame oil. Set aside.
  2. Whisk together soy sauce and sugar in a small bowl. Set aside.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook and stir carrots, asparagus, onions, and garlic in hot oil until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in green onions and shiitake mushrooms. Continue cooking and stirring for 30 seconds.
  4. Pour in soy sauce mixture, then add noodles. Cook and stir until noodles are warmed through, 2 to 3 minutes. Remove from heat and toss with sesame seeds and remaining 1 1/2 teaspoons of sesame oil.
