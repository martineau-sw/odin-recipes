Kimchi

Description

Classic Korean side-dish made by fermenting cabbages is rich in probiotics

Ingredients

  • Napa Cabbage, 2 heads
  • Sea Salt, 1 1/4 cups
  • Fish Sauce, 1 tablespoon
  • 5 Green Onions, chopped
  • 1/2 Small White Onion, minced
  • Garlic, pressed, 2 cloves
  • White Sugar, 2 tablespoons
  • Ginger, ground, 1 teaspoon
  • Korean Chili Powder, 5 tablespoons

Directions

  1. Cut the cabbages in half lengthwise and trim the ends. Rinse and cut into pieces about 2 inch square. Place the cabbage into large reseable bags and sprinkle salt on the leaves so they are evenly coated. Use your hands to rub the salt in to the leaves. Seal the bags and leave at room temperature for 6 hours.
  2. Rinse the salt from the cabbage leaves and then drain and squeeze out any excess liquid. Place the cabbage in a large container with a tight fitting lid. Stir in the fish sauce, green onions, white onion, garlic, sugar, and ginger. Sprinkle the Korean chili powder over the mixture. Wear the plastic gloves to protect your hands and rub the chili powder into the cabbage leaves until evenly coated. Seal the container and set in a cool dry place. Leave undisturbed for days. Refrigerate before serving, and store in the refrigerator for up to 1 month.
